A bee dives into a lotus flower at Kenilworth Park and Aquatic Gardens in Washington, DC

May 20, 2021
If flowers are the starring cast of nature's big spring show, then bees are the crew. Honey bees are always buzzing in the background, pollinating not only our photogenic petaled friends, but also food crops that make up a third of humans' diet. But when seen in front of the camera, they're hardly bee-listers: Today's pollen-coated photo subject was certainly ready for her close-up.

Maybe she's striking a pose on the pink carpet for World Bee Day. The annual event is held each May 20 to celebrate bees' crucial environmental role and raise awareness of the threats they face, especially as the world bee population has declined in recent decades. By carrying pollen, our bee friend is helping fertilize this lotus and the many other flowering plants here at Kenilworth Park and Aquatic Gardens. This sprawling preserve protects 700 acres of tidal marshland in the far eastern corner of Washington, DC.
